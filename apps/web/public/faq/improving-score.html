Improving Your Score

Convert audit findings into action. Discover which fixes have the biggest impact and how to prioritize improvements for maximum AI visibility gains.

Which fixes have the biggest impact?

Not all optimizations are created equal. Focus on these high-leverage improvements first:

1. Improve title clarity and brand presence HIGH IMPACT

Why: Titles are the first thing AI models parse. Clear, descriptive titles with your brand name improve entity recognition by 40-60%.

Action: Ensure every page has a unique, descriptive title under 65 characters that includes your brand. Format: "Primary Topic | Brand Name"

2. Add FAQ or HowTo structured data HIGH IMPACT

Why: FAQPage schema directly maps to how users query AI assistants. Pages with FAQ markup get cited 2-3x more often.

Action: Convert your existing FAQ content to JSON-LD FAQPage schema. Even 3-5 questions make a difference.

3. Write concise, question-based content HIGH IMPACT

Why: AI models favor content that directly answers questions. "Answer first" formatting dramatically improves citation likelihood.

Action: Lead every page/section with a clear answer or value proposition in the first 2-3 sentences. Avoid vague marketing copy.

4. Fix duplicate H1s and canonical issues MEDIUM IMPACT

Why: Duplicate H1 tags confuse AI models about your page's primary topic. Correct canonicals prevent content dilution.

Action: Ensure every page has exactly one H1. Set canonical tags to point to the preferred URL version.

5. Build domain mentions on authoritative sources MEDIUM IMPACT

Why: AI models weight citations from high-authority domains (news, academic, government). Being mentioned alongside trusted sources boosts your credibility.

Action: Pursue press coverage, guest posts on industry publications, and partnerships that result in natural mentions.

Pro tip: Address High impact issues first. A single high-impact fix can improve your score more than 10 low-impact ones. Use your audit's "Fix First" section to prioritize.

How long until I see changes?

Realistic timeline: 2-8 weeks for most improvements.

AI models don't refresh instantly. Here's what affects timing:

  • Immediate (within hours): Technical fixes like robots.txt, canonical tags, and schema markup are detected on next crawl
  • 2-4 weeks: Content improvements, new FAQ pages, and answerability changes show up as models re-index
  • 4-8 weeks: Citation frequency increases and authority signals compound. This is when you'll see material score improvements
  • 3-6 months: Domain-level authority improvements (backlinks, press mentions) take longer to propagate

Key insight: AI visibility is a compounding metric. Early improvements create momentum—each citation makes the next one more likely.

Does Optiview re-score automatically?

No—you need to trigger audits manually or set up recurring scans.

Why: Audits are computationally expensive (we crawl pages, render JavaScript, test AI citations). Automatic scoring would quickly exceed infrastructure limits.

Recommended schedule:

  • Weekly: If actively fixing issues
  • Monthly: For ongoing monitoring
  • After major updates: Content launches, site redesigns, schema additions

Pro accounts: Get automated monthly audits and trend tracking over time.

Quick wins: What can I fix today?

These improvements take less than an hour and yield immediate benefits:

  1. Add Organization schema to your homepage
    Copy a template, fill in your brand details, and add to <head>. ⏱ 10 minutes
  2. Fix duplicate H1 tags
    Use browser DevTools to find pages with multiple H1s and consolidate to one primary heading. ⏱ 15 minutes
  3. Ensure AI crawlers can access your site
    Check robots.txt and remove any Disallow rules blocking GPTBot, Claude-Web, etc. ⏱ 5 minutes
  4. Add FAQ schema to your help/support page
    Convert 3-5 existing FAQs to JSON-LD format. ⏱ 20 minutes
  5. Rewrite vague hero sections
    Replace "We're revolutionizing X" with "We help [audience] do [outcome] through [method]." ⏱ 10 minutes per page

After implementing quick wins: Run another audit to measure impact. You should see 5-15 point improvements immediately.

Common mistakes that hurt AI visibility

Avoid these pitfalls that actively damage your score:

  1. Blocking AI crawlers in robots.txt
    If you've disallowed GPTBot, Claude-Web, or CCBot, you're invisible to those models. Only block if you have a specific business reason.
  2. JavaScript-only content rendering
    If critical content only appears after JavaScript executes, some AI crawlers will miss it. Ensure server-side rendering or static HTML fallbacks.
  3. Keyword stuffing in titles and descriptions
    AI models penalize unnatural repetition. Write for humans first, machines second.
  4. Orphan pages with no internal links
    Pages not linked from your sitemap or other pages are effectively invisible. Build a strong internal linking structure.
  5. Ignoring mobile optimization
    Many AI crawlers prioritize mobile-friendly content. Poor mobile experience = lower visibility.
