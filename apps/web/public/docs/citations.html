Understanding Citations & AEO/GEO

A comprehensive guide to how Optiview tracks and measures your visibility in AI search engines.

TL;DR: AEO = AI engines citing YOUR site. GEO = Google/Bing AI citing YOUR site. Organic = Traditional search + competitor references.

What Are Citations?

A citation is when an AI search engine includes a link to a website as a source for its answer. Citations are the new "ranking" in the AI search era.

Example: Traditional Search vs. AI Search

Traditional Google Search:

User searches "what is YourProduct" → Google shows 10 blue links → User clicks #1

AI Search (Brave AI, ChatGPT, Perplexity):

User asks "what is YourProduct" → AI generates answer → AI cites 3-5 sources → User reads answer (may or may not click)

Key Difference: In AI search, being cited in the answer is more valuable than ranking #1 in traditional results.

The Three Citation Types

AEO Answer Engine Optimization

Definition: Citations from pure AI answer engines that generate responses using LLMs.

Sources:

  • ✅ Brave AI (currently tracked)
  • 🔜 ChatGPT Search
  • 🔜 Perplexity AI
  • 🔜 You.com AI
  • 🔜 Claude with search

How It Works:

  1. User asks question to AI engine
  2. AI searches web, generates answer
  3. AI includes citations to sources
  4. We detect when YOUR domain is cited

Why It Matters: AEO citations show you're being recommended by AI as an authoritative source. This is the future of search discovery.

GEO Generative Engine Optimization

Definition: Citations from traditional search engines that added AI features.

Sources (coming soon):

  • 🔜 Google AI Overviews
  • 🔜 Bing Copilot
  • 🔜 Google SGE (Search Generative Experience)

How It Works:

  1. User searches on Google/Bing
  2. AI Overview box appears at top of results
  3. Overview cites 2-4 sources
  4. We detect when YOUR domain is in the overview

Why It Matters: GEO citations have massive reach (billions of Google users). Being in AI Overviews is the new "ranking #1".

Organic Traditional SEO

Definition: Regular search citations plus competitor/reference sites.

Includes:

  • Traditional search engine results (Googlebot, Bingbot crawls)
  • Competitor sites cited alongside yours in AI answers
  • Reference sites (Wikipedia, news, industry authorities)

Why It Matters: Shows the competitive landscape. Which competitors are AI engines citing? What topics do you need to cover better?

How Optiview Tracks Citations

1. Smart Query Generation

For each audit, we run 30 carefully crafted queries across 6 buckets:

  • Brand Core (4 queries): Your brand name, site:domain.com
  • Product How-To (3 queries): How to use, how it works
  • Jobs to Be Done (4 queries): Benefits, why use, for patients
  • Schema Probes (7 queries): FAQ, eligibility, coverage, cost
  • Content Seeds (10 queries): Page titles as questions
  • Competitive (2 queries): Alternatives, comparisons

2. Source Collection

Each query returns ~10 sources from Brave AI. We collect:

  • Source URL
  • Which query found it
  • Position in results
  • Whether it matches your domain

3. Citation Classification

For each source, we determine:

if (source.domain === your_domain) {
  type = "AEO"  // You're being cited!
} else {
  type = "Organic"  // Competitor or reference site
}

4. Results Display

In the Citations tab, you see:

  • All (312): Every source from every query
  • AEO (91): Only sources citing YOUR domain
  • GEO (0): Google/Bing AI (coming soon)
  • Organic (221): Competitors + traditional search

Interpreting Your Citation Data

What's a "Good" AEO Count?

AEO Citations Assessment Action
0-20 🔴 Poor Critical: Optimize basics (robots.txt, structured data, content)
20-50 🟡 Moderate Improve: Add FAQ schemas, expand content, target more queries
50-100 🟢 Good Maintain: Keep creating quality content, monitor competitors
100+ 🟢 Excellent Lead: You're dominating AI search for your niche!

Key Metrics to Watch

  • Domain-Hit Rate: % of queries that cited your domain at least once. Higher = broader topic coverage.
  • Pages Cited: Number of unique pages cited. Higher = more of your content is valuable to AI.
  • Avg Citations Per Page: AEO count ÷ Pages Cited. Higher = certain pages are being cited multiple times.
  • Zero-Result Queries: Queries that didn't cite you. These are optimization opportunities!

Using Citation Data to Improve

1. Find Content Gaps

Look at queries with "View (0)" in the Brave AI modal. These are topics where:

  • AI couldn't find relevant content on your site
  • Competitors are dominating
  • You have an opportunity to create new content

2. Optimize High-Performing Pages

In the Pages tab, sort by "AI (Brave)" to see which pages are cited most. Make these even better:

  • Add more detailed answers
  • Include FAQ schema
  • Add How-To schema
  • Link to related content

3. Study Competitor Citations

Filter Citations to Organic to see which competitors AI engines cite. Learn from them:

  • What topics are they covering that you're not?
  • How are they structuring their content?
  • What schemas are they using?

4. Track Progress Over Time

Re-run audits monthly to see if your AEO count improves. Look for:

  • Increasing AEO citations (your content is resonating with AI)
  • More pages cited (broader content coverage)
  • Higher domain-hit rate (answering more query types)
